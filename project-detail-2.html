Overview

ITASE 6.0 is an annual national-level Information Technology competition held in Purwokerto. I entered the UX Design category and designed Serenity — a mobile wellbeing app for university students experiencing academic stress. The project covered the full UX design lifecycle, from user research through to a polished interactive prototype.

Design Process
01

Empathize

Conducted 8 semi-structured user interviews and distributed a survey to 40+ university students to understand stress patterns, coping mechanisms, and existing tool gaps.

02

Define

Synthesized research into two primary personas and identified a core HMW: "How might we help students build micro-wellness habits without disrupting their study flow?"

03

Ideate

Ran solo crazy-8s sessions and mapped out an affinity diagram of 60+ ideas, narrowing to 3 concept directions before selecting the habit-stack companion model.

04

Prototype

Built low-fi wireframes in Figma, iterated through two rounds of paper prototype testing, then produced a fully interactive hi-fi prototype with micro-animations.

05

Test

Usability tested with 5 participants (think-aloud protocol). Measured task completion, SUS score (84 — Excellent), and collected qualitative feedback for iteration.

Snapshots
Key Learnings

Constraints sharpen creativity

A 48-hour design sprint forced ruthless prioritisation — every feature had to earn its place through research evidence.

Micro-interactions matter

Small, delightful animations in the prototype significantly boosted perceived usability in user testing (SUS +12 vs flat prototype).

Present the story, not the screens

Judges responded most strongly to the narrative arc — connecting research insight → design decision → validated outcome.
